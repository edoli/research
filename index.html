Daniel S. Jeon (전석준) is a PhD student in the Visual Computing Lab, KAIST. He received his MS degree in computer science from KAIST in 2016. Prior to coming to the VCLAB, KAIST, he obtained his BS degree in computer science from KAIST. His research interests include computational imaging, optics, hyperspectral imaging, BRDF acquisition, and computer graphics. Specifically, he has developed various camera systems and algorithms for high-dimensional and high-resolution imaging. Also, he has exploited deep neural networks to learn hyperspectral imaging with compressive sensing and image super-resolution for stereo imaging. His current research focuses on developing a learning-based high-performance imaging system using diffractive optics.
